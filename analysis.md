## Biggest Current Design Problems
1. **Color dissonance** — electric blue-violet `#3225BA` fights against unbranded grey backgrounds and a separate secondary blue `#188bf6`. Reads busy, not disciplined.
2. **Weak hero hierarchy** — huge H2 stacked above a smaller H1, generic body copy, unclear primary CTA.
3. **No real instructor photos** — instructor page uses SVG placeholders with "READ BIO" buttons that don't open bios on the page.
4. **Review section repeats on every page** — same 6 reviews, every page, feels templated.
5. **Booking flow is a single long form** — no staged 2-step funnel, no program selector, no trust anchoring above the fold on the booking step.
6. **Program pages are thin** — each program has one paragraph of copy and no supporting structure (no schedule, no who-it's-for, no FAQ).

## Design Recommendation
**`performance-athletic-skill`** — bold sportswear-brand energy, kinetic marquees, one electric accent.

**Why this style for 5150:**
- They serve a **broad family audience** (3-year-olds through adult competitors) — cinematic-fight would be too dark, championship-heritage would be too legacy, brutalist would feel too hardcore for kids' parents.
- They have a **modern, accessible Gracie program + kickboxing** — performance-athletic matches that "train like an athlete, look like a brand you'd wear" feel.
- They don't surface a **competitive fighter roster** (no active pros listed), so cinematic-fight's pro-team framing would overclaim.
- They don't surface **multi-decade lineage or championship heritage** prominently, so championship-heritage would also overclaim.
- Performance-athletic lets the electric blue-violet accent (`#3225BA`, already their brand color) sing against a disciplined dark substrate + white negative space.

## Brand Color Direction (feeding into Step 3)
- **Keep:** `#3225BA` as the signal accent — it IS the brand and it's strong against dark.
- **Reframe the base:** move off unbranded grey `#F5F5F5` — too template-y. Replace with near-black obsidian substrate + pure white content surfaces.
- **Drop:** the secondary blue `#188bf6` — it fights `#3225BA` and adds noise without adding brand recognition.
