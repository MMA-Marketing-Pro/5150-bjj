The trial class, minute by minute

Here's exactly what happens when you walk in.

We've heard every version of "I don't want my kid to feel stupid on day one." Here's the actual sequence so you know what you're walking into.

  1. 00:00

    Walk in — we've got you.

    Come 15 minutes early. The front desk will expect you by name. A coach grabs a loaner gi in their size. You don't buy anything.

  2. 00:05

    Professor introduces themselves.

    Eye-level with your kid. "Hey, I'm Professor Scott. What's your name? Ever done this before?" No hype. Just warmth.

  3. 00:15

    Warmup game — it looks like tag.

    They're smiling by minute three. You're watching from a bench 20 feet away. We built this part so new kids don't feel singled out.

  4. 00:30

    Technique of the day.

    One move, broken down into three steps. Paired with a matched-size kid. A coach floats between every pair so nobody is lost.

  5. 00:50

    Light live round.

    Age-appropriate, controlled. Your kid finds out they can actually do the move. This is the moment parents text us about later.

  6. 01:00

    5-minute chat — no pressure.

    We answer your questions. If your kid loved it, we'll show you the schedule. If they didn't, we shake your hand and wave you out. No hard sell. Ever.

The questions every parent asks us

Before you book — yes, we've thought of that.

Is Jiu-Jitsu actually safe for a 6-year-old?

Safer than soccer, safer than skateboarding, safer than a trampoline park. Kids BJJ is all ground-based grappling on 2-inch mats — no punching, no kicking, no head contact. The most common "injury" is a hair tie getting caught in a sleeve. Every class is coach-supervised and size-matched.

What if my kid is shy and doesn't know anyone?

Every kid in the room was brand new at one point. We pair shy kids with our most patient assistant coaches for the first week. Parents regularly tell us their shy kid came out of the first class asking when they can come back — because nobody made it weird.

What if my kid is TOO rough and I'm worried they'll hurt someone?

That's actually the perfect kid for this program. BJJ teaches controlled force — how to use leverage instead of muscle, and how to stop when someone taps. Rough kids usually turn into the most respectful students on the mat within 60 days, because they finally have a place where their physicality is a useful tool instead of a problem.

Do we need to buy a gi and all that gear before we show up?

No. Show up in a t-shirt and shorts — we'll have a clean loaner gi in their size waiting. If they love it and you sign up for a membership, we'll help you pick a gi that actually fits. We don't upsell you gear on day one.

How many classes a week do they need?

Two is the minimum for real progress. Three is where most of our kids live — enough to sleep well, see measurable improvement, and stay ahead of belt promotions. Four or more is for kids who start competing. We'll help you pick a realistic schedule after the trial.

What does it cost after the trial?

We'll walk you through pricing in person after the trial class — because it depends on how many classes per week, family sibling rates, and whether you want gi included. Membership is month-to-month with no long-term contracts. You're never locked in.

What if they try it once and hate it?

Then they hate it, and you go home. The trial is free. We don't call you three times, we don't guilt you, we don't put you on a list. Our goal is to put the right kids on the mat — not every kid.
