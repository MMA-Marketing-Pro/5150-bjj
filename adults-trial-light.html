Your first class, minute by minute

Here's exactly what happens when you walk in.

Walking into a Jiu-Jitsu gym for the first time at 38 takes more nerve than the class itself. So here's the whole thing, start to finish, no surprises.

  1. 00:00

    Walk in. We've got you.

    Come 15 minutes early. The front desk expects you by name and a clean loaner gi in your size is already set aside. You don't buy anything.

  2. 00:05

    Meet your professor.

    A handshake, not a drill sergeant. "Ever trained before? Anything banged up we should work around?" That's the whole interrogation.

  3. 00:10

    Warm up with the class.

    Movement drills and partner work at your own pace. Nobody singles you out, and nobody cares that you're new. Every person on that mat had a day one.

  4. 00:25

    Technique of the day.

    One move, broken into steps, drilled with a partner your size. A coach floats between pairs so you're never lost and never guessing.

  5. 00:45

    Controlled rounds.

    Positional sparring at the pace you choose. Want to keep drilling on day one? Fine. Want to try a light roll? Also fine. This is the part people get hooked on.

  6. 01:00

    Five-minute chat. No pressure.

    We answer your questions. If you loved it, we'll show you the schedule and pricing. If you didn't, we shake your hand and wave you out. No hard sell. Ever.

The questions every adult asks us

Before you book: yes, we've thought of that.

I'm in my 30s/40s and out of shape. Am I too old to start?

No. Most adults who walk through our door are 30 to 45 and haven't trained anything in years. Here's the part the internet gets backwards: you don't get in shape to start Jiu-Jitsu. You start Jiu-Jitsu to get in shape. The class is the conditioning, you set your own pace, and nobody expects you to keep up with the 25-year-olds on day one.

Will I get hurt? Be honest.

Honest answer: it's contact training, so we won't pretend it's pilates. But your first classes are controlled drilling and positional work with size-matched, experienced partners, not hard sparring. We teach you to tap early and our culture enforces it. The injuries we actually see at the beginner level are the gym-class variety: a jammed finger, and muscles you forgot you had reminding you they exist.

I have zero experience and I don't want to look stupid.

Every single person on that mat had a day one, and they all remember it. New adults are paired with our most patient training partners, and the technique is broken down step by step so you're never left guessing. And honestly? Nobody is watching you. They're too busy trying to survive their own rounds.

Do I need a gi or any gear before I show up?

No. Come in a t-shirt and shorts with a water bottle, and we'll have a clean loaner gi in your size waiting. If you join, we'll point you to a gi that actually fits. Nobody upsells you gear on day one.

How often should I train, and when do adults actually train?

Two classes a week is the minimum for real progress. Three is the sweet spot most of our adults settle into. Adult classes run at 12:00 PM Monday and Wednesday, 7:15 PM Monday through Thursday, and 11:00 AM Saturday, so there's a slot whether you're a lunch-break trainer or an after-work regular.

What does it cost after the free trial?

We walk you through pricing in person after your trial, because it depends on how many classes per week you want and whether family members train too. Membership is month-to-month with no long-term contracts. You're never locked in.

What if I try it and it's just not for me?

Then you finally know, and that question stops following you around. The trial is free. We don't call you three times, we don't guilt you, we don't put you on a list. Our goal is the right adults on the mat, not every adult.
